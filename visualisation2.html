Exploring Global Population in 2021

for the Most and Least Populated Countries

Background Information

Over time the main trend for the total world population is a postive trend, where the global population is continuously increasing. It increases by about 83 million people per year. However, it is beleived that as the years continue, the total world population will be increasing at a slower rate or even reaching its peak. This is an interesting and potentially surprising fact.

In 2021, the approximated total population reached nearly 8 billion.

Visualisation Information

From the map, it can be seen that countries all have different population numbers, therefore they all contribute to the global population differently.

This visualisation explores how aspects like life expectancy, death, birth and fertility rates differ between the most and least populated countries. It explores if the differences in those aspects could explain why there is a difference in population numbers. It also looks into if there are certain trends between the most populated countries and trends between the least populated countries.
